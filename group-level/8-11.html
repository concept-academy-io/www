Grades 3-5 (Ages 8-11)

Applied Math
  • Arithmetic Mastery:
    • Multiply/Divide 100 - Practice multiplication and division within 100.
    • Factors/Multiples Intro - Understand the concepts of factors and multiples.
    • Four Operations - Tackle multistep problems using all four basic operations.
  • Geometry Advancement:
    • Angles & Polygons - Learn about angles and polygonal shapes.
    • Perimeter/Area - Calculate perimeter and area of basic shapes.
    • Volume Intro - Grasp the basics of volume measurement.
  • Applied Math - Cash Box
    • Real-life Math - Apply math concepts in a simulated classroom store.
    • Revenue/Expenses - Track and analyze income and costs.
Concept Prototyping
  • Mechanical Crafts - Create moving art with water wheels and other handmade mechanisms.
  • Thingiverse Exploration - Investigate open-source innovations and spark your own creativity.
  • CAD - Level up your design skills with pro tools like TinkerCAD.
  • Electrical Circuits - Illuminate the basics of electricity with hands-on Snap Circuit projects.
  • Forces and Motion - Unearth the secrets of gravity, friction, and motion through fun experiments.
Craftsmanship Commercialization
  • Mechanical Design - Employs hands-on projects to create small devices, like windmills or miniature catapults, to understand mechanical systems.
  • Basic Circuitry - Teaches electrical fundamentals through projects like creating simple circuits and understanding electrical flow.
Digital Media
  • Introduction to File Management - Master folder creation, file sorting, and backup.
  • Privacy Basics - Learn secure passwords and privacy settings.
  • Make a Simple Blog Post - Write and publish a blog post with photos.
  • Tech: Google Docs, Blogger - Learn the basics of Google Docs and Blogger.
Global Studies
Un-ordered Listing due to Interdependence and Age Appropriateness
  • Continents and Tectonic Plates - Examines the movement and formation of the Earth's crust.
  • Countries - All students must learn all the countires of the world and be able to locate them on a blank map.
  • Simple Resources - Overview of essential natural elements and how they sustain life.
  • Global Resources - Discusses the management and sustainability of worldwide resources.
  • Global Transportation - Studies the logistics and environmental impact of international transport systems.
  • Global Trade - Focuses on economic and policy aspects of trade between nations.
  • Types of Government - Compares democratic, authoritarian, and other governance systems.
  • Basic Religions - Introduces core tenets and practices of major world religions.
  • Politics, Beliefs and Religion - Analyzes how politics and faith intersect and influence each other.
  • Basic Conflicts - Investigates the common causes and solutions for disputes at different scales.
  • In-depth Study of Conflicts - Delves into historical and ongoing global conflicts, examining case studies.
Human Biology
  • Basic Organs - Investigate functions and importance of organs like the heart, lungs, and kidneys.
  • Muscular & Nervous System - Study how muscles work with nerves for movement and sensation.
  • Brain Development - Learn how the brain matures and affects learning and behavior.
  • Peer Groups & Social Influence - Analyze how friends and groups can affect decisions and feelings.
  • Sexual Anatomy - Introduce primary and secondary sexual characteristics.
  • Puberty Changes - Understand physical, emotional, and hormonal changes during puberty.
Linguistic Communication
  • Cursive - Add flair to your writing by learning cursive script.
  • Effective Messaging - Become a pro at getting your point across.
  • Advanced Reading - Tackle challenging texts for enriched vocabulary.
  • Tech: Email, Google Docs, Blogger - Communicate like a pro using digital tools.
  • Create a Comic Strip - Blend art and narrative in your own comic adventures.
  • News Reporter - Hone clarity and focus by acting as a junior reporter.
Music Platforms
  • Instrument Proficiency - Advanced lessons on preferred instruments.
  • Music Composition - Introduction to writing simple tunes and melodies.
  • Recording Techniques - Explore multi-track recording and basic mixing.
Transactional Wealth
  • Money & Credit - Explore the role and management of money and credit.
  • Basic Banking - Get acquainted with banking services and account management.
  • Introduction to Budgeting - Master the basics of income and expense planning.
All Grades: Foundational Requisites
Only returning students may enroll into grades 9-12 due to these foundational knowlege requirements.
Global Studies Co-Requisites
  • Know all continents
  • Know all countries
  • Know the distribution of vial Global Resources
  • Know the Global Population Distributions
  • Know the Global Transportation Routes
  • Know Global Language Distributions
  • Logic Patterns - Understand reasoning through modus ponens and modus tollens.
  • Know the Blue Zones (optional)
  • Know the Periodic Table (optional)
General Co-Requisites
  • Typing
  • Penmanship & Handwriting
  • Arduino Board Layout
  • Arduino-C Language Subset
MEP Suplements
Due to the focus of our educational program, we have a streamlined minimalistic implementation of:
Domestic Civics as well as Academic Chemistry & Physics.
MEP Domestic Civics
  • Local & National Landmarks
  • Basic Economics: Needs vs Wants
  • State Government
MEP Academic Chemistry
  • Introduction to Elements and Compounds
  • Chemical Symbols and Formulas
  • Chemical Reactions: Combining and Breaking Apart
  • pH Levels: Acids and Bases
MEP Academic Physics
  • Magnetism: Poles and Fields
  • Heat: Conduction, Convection, Radiation
  • Simple Electrical Circuits
