Grades 1-2 (Ages 6-8)

Applied Math
  • AM-06: Basic Skill Refinement:
    • Count to 100 - Expand number recognition and counting up to 100.
    • Add/Subtract 100 - Strengthen addition and subtraction skills within 100.
    • Time & Money - Introduce time-telling and basic money concepts.
  • AM-07: Geometry Fundamentals:
    • 3D Shapes - Learn about three-dimensional shapes like cubes and spheres.
    • Simple Fractions - Understand simple fractions using shapes.
    • Spatial Reasoning - Solve puzzles that require understanding of space.
Concept Prototyping
  • Gears, Levers, and Pulleys - Dive into the magic of simple machines that power our world.
  • Makey Makey Basics - Transform everyday objects into touchpads and interactive surfaces.
  • 3D Design - Sculpt your dreams into reality with advanced 3D design techniques.
  • Arduino and LEDs - Light up your world with beginner-friendly Arduino LED projects.
Craftsmanship Commercialization
  • Simple Machines - Introduces levers, pulleys, and wheels to demonstrate how force is applied in everyday life.
  • Basic Coding - Uses Scratch and other block-based languages to teach foundational programming logic.
Digital Media
  • Understanding Digital Media - Discover image, video, and audio formats.
  • Drawings to Digital Art - Learn to scan and color sketches on a computer.
  • Tech: Tablets, Kid-friendly apps - Use educational apps for both learning and play.
  • Monthly Highlights - Compile a digital album that captures your activities.
Global Studies
Un-ordered Listing due to Interdependence and Age Appropriateness
  • Continents and Tectonic Plates - Examines the movement and formation of the Earth's crust.
  • Countries - All students must learn all the countires of the world and be able to locate them on a blank map.
  • Simple Resources - Overview of essential natural elements and how they sustain life.
  • Global Resources - Discusses the management and sustainability of worldwide resources.
  • Global Transportation - Studies the logistics and environmental impact of international transport systems.
  • Global Trade - Focuses on economic and policy aspects of trade between nations.
  • Types of Government - Compares democratic, authoritarian, and other governance systems.
  • Basic Religions - Introduces core tenets and practices of major world religions.
  • Politics, Beliefs and Religion - Analyzes how politics and faith intersect and influence each other.
  • Basic Conflicts - Investigates the common causes and solutions for disputes at different scales.
  • In-depth Study of Conflicts - Delves into historical and ongoing global conflicts, examining case studies.
Human Biology
  • Skeletal System - Examine the bones in the human body and their functions.
  • Growth and Development - Explore the physical and emotional changes during early childhood.
  • Personal Boundaries - Learn the importance of personal space and verbal/non-verbal cues.
  • Good Touch / Bad Touch - Recognize characteristics of trustworthy adults and friends.
Linguistic Communication
  • Reading and Penmanship - Enhance reading skills while perfecting your handwriting.
  • Basic Grammar - Nail the building blocks of well-structured sentences.
  • Tech: Tablets, Kid-friendly apps - Navigate apps designed for fun and education.
  • Storytelling with Pictures - Illustrate your stories to make them come alive.
  • Emoji Sentences - Express emotions and ideas using fun emojis.
Music Platforms
  • Instrument Basics - Learn the essentials of instruments like piano, drums, and flute.
  • Rhythm & Melody - Understand the fundamentals of musical patterns.
  • Recording Intro - Get hands-on with basic recording apps and devices.
Transactional Wealth
  • Barter - Understand the history and mechanics of trade without money.
  • Basic Trade - Grasp the fundamentals of trading goods and services.
  • Simple Budgeting - Learn to manage finances with basic budgeting techniques.
All Grades: Foundational Requisites
Only returning students may enroll into grades 10-12 due to these foundational knowlege requirements.
Global Studies Co-Requisites
  • Know all continents
  • Know all countries
  • Know the distribution of vial Global Resources
  • Know the Global Population Distributions
  • Know the Global Transportation Routes
  • Know Global Language Distributions
  • Logic Patterns - Understand reasoning through modus ponens and modus tollens.
  • Know the Blue Zones (optional)
  • Know the Periodic Table (optional)
General Co-Requisites
  • Typing
  • Penmanship & Handwriting
  • Arduino Board Layout
  • Arduino-C Language Subset
