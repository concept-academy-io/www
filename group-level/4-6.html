Pre-K to K (Ages 4-6)

Applied Math
  • AM-01: Foundation Building:
    • Count to 100 - Learn to recognize and count numbers up to 100.
    • Basic Shapes - Understand fundamental geometric shapes and spatial reasoning.
    • Patterns Intro - Engage in basic pattern recognition exercises.
  • AM-02: Early Problem-Solving:
    • Basic Add/Subtract - Practice arithmetic with hands-on activities.
    • Compare Quantities - Learn to compare numbers in different sets.
    • Non-standard Measurement - Measure items using unconventional units.
Concept Prototyping
  • LEGO and Basic Structures - Unleash your creativity by building amazing structures with LEGO.
  • Mechanical Toys - Discover the joy and mechanics behind wind-up toys, cars, and more.
  • Tech Comfort - Gain confidence in navigating educational apps to enhance learning.
  • Kid-Friendly CAD - Turn your ideas into 3D models with easy-to-use TinkerCAD.
Craftsmanship Commercialization
  • First Strokes - Introduction to drawing with crayons and watercolors.
  • Simple Crafts - Crafting with paper, clay, and recyclable materials.
Digital Media
  • Introduction to Photos and Videos - Learn to capture and view photos and videos on basic devices.
  • Basic Computer Skills - Hands-on tutorials for keyboard, mouse, and basic interface use.
  • Tech: Tablets, Kids Camera - Practice on kid-friendly tablets and cameras with guided activities.
  • Monthly Highlights - Create a monthly digital scrapbook to capture memories.
Global Studies
Un-ordered Listing due to Interdependence and Age Appropriateness
  • Continents and Tectonic Plates - Examines the movement and formation of the Earth's crust.
  • Countries - All students must learn all the countires of the world and be able to locate them on a blank map.
  • Simple Resources - Overview of essential natural elements and how they sustain life.
  • Global Resources - Discusses the management and sustainability of worldwide resources.
  • Global Transportation - Studies the logistics and environmental impact of international transport systems.
  • Global Trade - Focuses on economic and policy aspects of trade between nations.
  • Types of Government - Compares democratic, authoritarian, and other governance systems.
  • Basic Religions - Introduces core tenets and practices of major world religions.
  • Politics, Beliefs and Religion - Analyzes how politics and faith intersect and influence each other.
  • Basic Conflicts - Investigates the common causes and solutions for disputes at different scales.
  • In-depth Study of Conflicts - Delves into historical and ongoing global conflicts, examining case studies.
Human Biology
  • Food Groups - Discover the roles of proteins, fats, and carbohydrates in health.
  • Body Parts - Identify and label body parts, learning their names in multiple languages.
  • Touch Awareness - Distinguish between types of touch and their appropriateness.
Linguistic Communication
  • Basic Communication - Master the essentials of verbal and non-verbal cues.
  • Reading: Alphabet Recognition - Start your literacy journey by mastering the ABCs.
  • Tech: Tablets, Kids Camera - Get comfy with kid-friendly gadgets and cameras.
Music Platforms
  • Making Noise - Introduction to basic instruments and turning noise into tunes.
  • Digital Fun - Explore apps for creating music and beats.
Transactional Wealth
  • Economics of Sharing - When someone in your circle has, gains, or succeeds, it enriches the whole community. Emphasizes the mutual benefits of sharing resources and knowledge for mutual benefit.
All Grades: Foundational Requisites
Only returning students may enroll into grades 9-12 due to these foundational knowlege requirements.
Global Studies Co-Requisites
  • Know all continents
  • Know all countries
  • Know the distribution of vial Global Resources
  • Know the Global Population Distributions
  • Know the Global Transportation Routes
  • Know Global Language Distributions
  • Logic Patterns - Understand reasoning through modus ponens and modus tollens.
  • Know the Blue Zones (optional)
  • Know the Periodic Table (optional)
General Co-Requisites
  • Typing
  • Penmanship & Handwriting
  • Arduino Board Layout
  • Arduino-C Language Subset
MEP Suplements
Due to the focus of our educational program, we have a streamlined minimalistic implementation of:
Domestic Civics as well as Academic Chemistry & Physics.
MEP Domestic Civics
  • What is a Community?
  • Simple Rules & Laws
  • Basic Needs: Food, Shelter, Clothing
MEP Academic Chemistry
  • Introduction to Science: Observing and Describing
  • Basic States of Matter: Solid, Liquid, Gas
  • Mixing Colors: Intro to Chemical Reactions
MEP Academic Physics
  • Understanding Motion: Fast and Slow
  • Basic Forces: Push and Pull
  • Simple Machines: Ramps and Levers
