Global Studies

Please see Foundations.

All Students

Our curriculum aims to equip students with the analytical skills necessary to critically evaluate information, especially in the context of global media. By fostering a nuanced understanding of international relations, resource management, and geopolitical strategies, we empower students to discern the underlying motives behind diplomatic and political actions. This ensures that they become informed, skeptical consumers of news, capable of independent thought and judgment.

Foundational knowledge is essential for progressing to higher-grade levels. Therefore, these topics are not tied to specific grades. They are taught and expanded upon in a manner suitable for each age group as student's progress toward graduation.

Un-ordered Listing due to Interdependence and Age Appropriateness
  • Continents and Tectonic Plates - Examines the movement and formation of the Earth's crust.
  • Countries - All students must learn all the countires of the world and be able to locate them on a blank map.
  • Simple Resources - Overview of essential natural elements and how they sustain life.
  • Global Resources - Discusses the management and sustainability of worldwide resources.
  • Global Transportation - Studies the logistics and environmental impact of international transport systems.
  • Global Trade - Focuses on economic and policy aspects of trade between nations.
  • Types of Government - Compares democratic, authoritarian, and other governance systems.
  • Basic Religions - Introduces core tenets and practices of major world religions.
  • Politics, Beliefs and Religion - Analyzes how politics and faith intersect and influence each other.
  • Basic Conflicts - Investigates the common causes and solutions for disputes at different scales.
  • In-depth Study of Conflicts - Delves into historical and ongoing global conflicts, examining case studies.
