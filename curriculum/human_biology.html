Human Biology & Sex Ed

From Pre-K to Grade 12

This robust biology and health curriculum is meticulously crafted to offer a comprehensive educational journey from Pre-K through Grade 12. Beginning with the essentials like basic anatomy, hygiene, and nutrition in early childhood, students gain the knowledge to make informed health choices.

In Grades 3-5, the focus shifts to understanding the building blocks of life, from cells to organs, and how these systems interact to sustain life. These fundamental concepts are linked to everyday health and well-being, enabling practical applications such as basic first aid and dietary choices. During middle school, students are introduced to more complex topics like genetics, organ systems, and physical development, forming a solid foundation for understanding the human body and personal health.

As they transition to Grades 10-12, learners engage with intricate topics like hormones, the immune system, and reproductive health. They also explore pressing issues such as mental health, substance awareness, and the impact of social media on self-image. The curriculum incorporates modern challenges like cyber safety, effective communication, and consent, thus preparing students for real-world scenarios. This multi-faceted approach ensures that the biology and health curriculum is not only deeply interconnected with scientific understanding but also socially relevant, making education both meaningful and practical.

Pre-K to K (Ages 4-6)
  • Food Groups - Discover the roles of proteins, fats, and carbohydrates in health.
  • Body Parts - Identify and label body parts, learning their names in multiple languages.
  • Touch Awareness - Distinguish between types of touch and their appropriateness.
Grades 1-2 (Ages 6-8)
  • Skeletal System - Examine the bones in the human body and their functions.
  • Growth and Development - Explore the physical and emotional changes during early childhood.
  • Personal Boundaries - Learn the importance of personal space and verbal/non-verbal cues.
  • Good Touch / Bad Touch - Recognize characteristics of trustworthy adults and friends.
Grades 3-5 (Ages 8-11)
  • Basic Organs - Investigate functions and importance of organs like the heart, lungs, and kidneys.
  • Muscular & Nervous System - Study how muscles work with nerves for movement and sensation.
  • Brain Development - Learn how the brain matures and affects learning and behavior.
  • Peer Groups & Social Influence - Analyze how friends and groups can affect decisions and feelings.
  • Sexual Anatomy - Introduce primary and secondary sexual characteristics.
  • Puberty Changes - Understand physical, emotional, and hormonal changes during puberty.
Grades 6-9 (Ages 11-15)
  • Genetics & Traits - Delve into how genes influence physical and behavioral traits.
  • Cell Biology - Explore cells as the building blocks of life, including organelles and functions.
  • Organ Systems - Understand how organs work together in systems like digestive, respiratory, and circulatory.
  • Teen Physical Development - Examine puberty, nutrition, and exercise impacts on physical growth.
  • Self Image & Social Media - Evaluate how online presence affects self-perception and body image.
  • Reproductive Anatomy - Discuss male and female reproductive systems in depth, including fertilization.
  • Fertility and Menstrual Cycle - Explore hormonal regulation and the menstrual cycle’s role in fertility.
  • Contraception - Study various birth control methods, effectiveness, and responsible use.
  • STD/STI Awareness - Recognize symptoms, prevention strategies, and treatments for STDs/STIs.
  • Cyber Safety - Discuss risks and laws regarding sexting and other unsafe online behaviors.
  • Consent and Boundaries - Learn how to establish and communicate personal boundaries.
  • Healthy Relationships - Explore elements of respectful, honest relationships.
  • Communication Skills - Develop effective ways to discuss sensitive or difficult topics.
  • Trusted Contacts - Learn how to identify and seek help from trustworthy individuals and organizations.
Grades 10-12 (Ages 15-18)
  • Nutrition and Digestion - Investigate the digestive process and nutritional needs for different activities.
  • Immune System - Learn about how the immune system fights off diseases and infections.
  • Hormones and Endocrine System - Study hormonal imbalances and effects on mood and development.
  • Reproductive System - Dive deep into reproductive anatomy, processes, and possible dysfunctions.
  • Health and Aging - Discuss how habits now will influence health in later years.
  • Emotions and Mental Health - Investigate the neuroscience behind emotions and mental well-being.
  • Substance Awareness - Examine effects and legal ramifications of drug and alcohol abuse.
  • Stress Management - Explore various coping mechanisms for handling stress effectively.
  • Media and Body Image - Evaluate media's impact on body image and self-esteem.
  • Self-Esteem and Mental Health - Understand the interrelation between self-esteem, mental health, and coping strategies.
  • Teen Pregnancy - Discuss options, consequences, and support systems related to teen pregnancy.
  • STD/STI Information - Explore identification, treatment, and prevention of various STDs/STIs.
  • Safe Practices - Learn about regular health check-ups, vaccinations, and open communication with healthcare providers.
  • Communication and Consent - Study legal and ethical aspects of consent in various situations.
  • Cyber Safety - Understand the long-term risks and potential legal issues with unsafe online behaviors.
  • Date Rape and Abuse - Learn to recognize red flags, preventive measures, and appropriate responses.
  • Effective Communication - Master techniques for respectful and assertive communication.
  • Trusted Contacts - Understand how to vet and select trustworthy contacts for personal and professional support.
