Music & Platforms

From Pre-K to Entrepreneurship

Our Music Platforms curriculum starts with 'Fundamentals of Music for Pre-K and K.' This age group enjoys simple sound-making activities and learns about basic instruments through interactive apps.

For Grades 1-2, the program introduces 'Instrument Basics' and 'Rhythm & Melody.' Students engage with foundational musical concepts, learn essential skills on various instruments like piano and drums, and start recording with simple apps and devices.

In Grades 3-5, titled 'Dabble & Discover,' students progress to 'Instrument Proficiency,' 'Music Composition,' and 'Recording Techniques.' This level focuses on advanced lessons for chosen instruments, introductory music writing, and multi-track recording basics.

Grades 6-8, under 'Refining the Craft,' delve into specialized instrument training and group projects for song production. An intro to media creation merges music with visuals for advanced projects.

High schoolers shift toward the business side. They learn advanced production, explore distribution methods, and discover how to monetize their work through online platforms.

The 'Entrepreneurship in Music' phase encourages viewing music as a marketable product, exploring the business dynamics of the music industry.

Fundamentals 1 Pre-K to K (Ages 4-6)
  • Making Noise - Introduction to basic instruments and turning noise into tunes.
  • Digital Fun - Explore apps for creating music and beats.
Fundamentals of Music Grades 1-2 (Ages 6-8)
  • Instrument Basics - Learn the essentials of instruments like piano, drums, and flute.
  • Rhythm & Melody - Understand the fundamentals of musical patterns.
  • Recording Intro - Get hands-on with basic recording apps and devices.
Dabble & Discover Grades 3-5 (Ages 8-11)
  • Instrument Proficiency - Advanced lessons on preferred instruments.
  • Music Composition - Introduction to writing simple tunes and melodies.
  • Recording Techniques - Explore multi-track recording and basic mixing.
Refining the Craft Grades 6-8 (Ages 11-14)
  • Advanced Instrumentation - Proficiency in chosen instrument or software.
  • Collaboration - Group projects for song production.
  • Introduction to Media - Basics of creating music videos and podcasts.
Monetizing Music Grades 9-12 (Ages 14-18)
  • Production Skills - Advanced music software and editing.
  • Branding &amp; Presence - Building an artist profile and imagery.
  • Distribution &amp; Monetization - Leveraging music platforms and royalties.
  • Promotion - Using social media and online advertising for music promotion.
Entrepreneurship in Music
Encourage students to view their music as a product and understand the music market dynamics.
