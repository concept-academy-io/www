Applied Math

From Pre-K to Grade 12

This comprehensive math curriculum is purposefully designed to be seamlessly integrated with various other courses, creating a meaningful and interconnected educational experience for students from Pre-K to Grade 12. Starting with the foundational understanding of numbers, shapes, and basic patterns in early years, students progress to mastering arithmetic and geometry skills in Grades 3-5. These mathematical foundations are not isolated concepts but are instead applied in real-life scenarios, such as financial mathematics and basic accounting principles, during middle school years.

As students move into Grades 6-8, they explore pre-algebra, algebra, and advanced geometry, acquiring problem-solving skills crucial for engineering applications. This bridge between math and engineering becomes evident in their understanding of 2D and 3D geometry and practical engineering concepts. In Grades 9-12, the curriculum delves into higher-level algebra and calculus, preparing students for advanced mathematical challenges. Furthermore, the inclusion of online marketing and statistics demonstrates the curriculum's adaptability to the digital age, enabling students to apply mathematical concepts directly to SEO/SEM strategies and online advertising algorithms. This intentional integration ensures that mathematical knowledge is not isolated but purposefully connected to various STEM disciplines and modern marketing practices, making learning more meaningful and relevant.

Pre-K to K (Ages 4-6)
  • AM-01: Foundation Building:
    • Count to 100 - Learn to recognize and count numbers up to 100.
    • Basic Shapes - Understand fundamental geometric shapes and spatial reasoning.
    • Patterns Intro - Engage in basic pattern recognition exercises.
  • AM-02: Early Problem-Solving:
    • Basic Add/Subtract - Practice arithmetic with hands-on activities.
    • Compare Quantities - Learn to compare numbers in different sets.
    • Non-standard Measurement - Measure items using unconventional units.
Grades 1-2 (Ages 6-8)
  • AM-06: Basic Skill Refinement:
    • Unit position values
    • Add/Subtract 100 - Strengthen addition and subtraction skills within 100.
    • Time - Teach students how to read analog and digital clocks, understand AM/PM, and calculate elapsed time.
    • Money - Introduce students to different denominations of currency, teach them how to make change, and basic budgeting skills.
  • AM-07: Geometry Fundamentals:
    • 3D Shapes - Learn about three-dimensional shapes like cubes and spheres.
    • Simple Fractions - Understand simple fractions using shapes.
    • Spatial Reasoning - Solve puzzles that require understanding of space such as tangram or Rubik's Cube.
Grades 3-5 (Ages 8-11)
  • Arithmetic Mastery:
    • Multiply/Divide 100 - Practice multiplication and division within 100.
    • Factors/Multiples Intro - Understand the concepts of factors and multiples.
    • Order of Operations - Tackle multistep problems using basic operations.
  • Geometry Advancement:
    • Angles & Polygons - Learn about angles and polygonal shapes.
    • Perimeter/Area - Calculate perimeter and area of basic shapes.
    • Volume Intro - Grasp the basics of volume measurement.
  • Applied Math - Cash Box
    • Real-life Math - Apply math concepts in a simulated classroom store.
    • Revenue/Expenses - Track and analyze income and costs.
Grades 6-8 (Ages 11-14)
  • Pre-Algebra and Algebra:
    • Integers/Rationals - Master operations with integers and rational numbers.
    • Algebra Intro - Learn algebraic expressions and simple equations.
    • Linear Equations - Solve linear equations and inequalities.
  • Geometry Mastery:
    • Advanced Geometry - Explore 2D and 3D geometric concepts.
    • Transformations - Understand geometric transformations and symmetry.
    • Complex Shapes - Calculate volume and surface area for complex shapes.
  • Applied Math - Engineering Concepts:
    • Math in Engineering - Apply math in structural and mechanical contexts.
    • Engineering Drawing - Learn basic drawing and measurement skills.
    • Prototypes - Create and test basic engineering prototypes.
Grades 9-12 (Ages 14-18)
  • Algebra II:
    • Quadratics/Exponentials - Explore quadratic and exponential functions.
    • Equation Systems - Work with systems of linear and nonlinear equations.
    • Trigonometry Intro - Learn basic trigonometry and advanced algebraic concepts.
  • Calculus:
    • Differentiation/Integration - Introduction to fundamental calculus topics.
    • Limits/Continuity - Explore mathematical limits and continuity.
  • Applied Math - Online Marketing and Statistics:
    • Marketing Stats - Master data collection and analysis in marketing.
    • SEO/SEM Stats - Apply statistical methods in SEO and SEM strategies.
    • Ad Algorithms - Understand the mathematics behind online ad algorithms.
