Engineering Education: From Childhood to Adulthood

For Grades 1-2 (Ages 6-8), we introduce Early Engineering. Young learners start with basic mechanical principles. Using hands-on activities like building simple structures and machines, they begin to understand forces, motion, and energy.

As students move on to Grades 3-5 (Ages 8-11), our curriculum emphasizes Building Foundations. At this stage, they delve into material science, learning about the different properties of construction materials. Students engage in projects that involve building model bridges, towers, and simple electrical circuits.

In Grades 6-8 (Ages 11-14), the curriculum focuses on Advanced Engineering Concepts. Students explore hydraulics, aerodynamics, and robotics. They take part in intricate projects such as designing water filtration systems and programming basic robots, thereby getting an introduction to code.

For Grades 9-12, the curriculum provides in-depth expertise in preferred mediums, portfolio building, and artistic branding. Students also learn the business aspects of art, including organizing exhibitions and sales.

In the final stage, the curriculum incorporates Art Entrepreneurship. This teaches high school students about the commercial aspects of art, including branding, copyright, and gallery partnerships.

Art Fundamentals: Pre-K to K (Ages 4-6)
  • First Strokes - Introduction to drawing with crayons and watercolors.
  • Simple Crafts - Crafting with paper, clay, and recyclable materials.
Early Engineering: Grades 1-2 (Ages 6-8)
  • Simple Machines - Introduces levers, pulleys, and wheels to demonstrate how force is applied in everyday life.
  • Basic Coding - Uses Scratch and other block-based languages to teach foundational programming logic.
Building Foundations: Grades 3-5 (Ages 8-11)
  • Mechanical Design - Employs hands-on projects to create small devices, like windmills or miniature catapults, to understand mechanical systems.
  • Basic Circuitry - Teaches electrical fundamentals through projects like creating simple circuits and understanding electrical flow.
Advanced Engineering Concepts: Grades 6-8 (Ages 11-14)
  • Robotics - Advances to building and programming robots, including basic AI behaviors.
  • Advanced Coding - Dives into Python and JavaScript, exploring data manipulation and web development.
Advanced Artistry: Grades 9-12 (Ages 14-18)
  • Mastering Mediums - In-depth studies in preferred art mediums.
  • Portfolio Building - Creating a diverse art portfolio.
  • Artistic Branding - Developing an artist's persona and self-presentation.
  • Exhibitions & Sales - Organizing art exhibits and understanding the business side.
  • Digital Presence - Building an online portfolio and using social media for promotion.
Art Entrepreneurship
High school students learn to brand their art, understand copyright, licensing, and gallery partnerships.
