Craftsmanship Commercialization

From Imagination to Market

Our Arts, Crafts & Commercialization curriculum commences with 'Fundamentals' for Pre-K and K. This age group explores colors, shapes, and basic crafting materials through hands-on activities, laying the groundwork for their creative journey.

In Grades 1-2, under 'Developing Skills,' the program ventures into 'Basic Handicrafts' and 'Introduction to Textile Arts.' Students begin to craft with a purpose, learning elementary techniques in knitting, sewing, and woodworking, while also being introduced to the world of textile arts.

'Dabble & Discover' is the theme for Grades 3-5, advancing to 'Exploration of Materials' and 'Introduction to Digital Art.' Here, students delve deeper into various materials and digital tools, expanding their creative horizons and starting to blend traditional crafts with digital artistry.

Grades 6-9, termed 'Refining the Craft,' sharpens skills with 'Advanced Handicraft Techniques' and 'Introduction to Digital Design.' Students now embark on more complex projects, refining their craftsmanship, and beginning to explore the digital realm of design and 3D modeling.

As students transition into high school, the focus shifts to 'Monetizing Crafts.' They learn 'Crafting for Commercialization,' 'Online Portfolio Creation,' and get introduced to 'Online Sales Platforms.' This phase gears them towards understanding the market, pricing their work, and exploring online sales venues.

The capstone, 'Entrepreneurship in Arts & Crafts,' empowers students to view their crafts as marketable products. They delve into the business dynamics within the arts and crafts industry, learning about branding, customer engagement, and business strategies - turning their creative passion into viable ventures.

Art Fundamentals: Pre-K to K (Ages 4-6)
  • First Strokes - Introduction to drawing with crayons and watercolors.
  • Simple Crafts - Crafting with paper, clay, and recyclable materials.
Developing Skills: Grades 1-2 (Ages 6-8)
  • Basic Handicrafts - Projects like knitting, sewing, and basic woodworking.
  • Introduction to Textile Arts - Exploring fabrics, threads, and basic weaving.
  • Recycled Art - Creating art from recycled materials.
Dabble & Discover: Grades 3-5 (Ages 8-11)
  • Exploration of Materials - Working with different materials like metals, plastics, and ceramics.
  • Basic Design Principles - Introducing elements and principles of design.
  • Introduction to Digital Art - Basic graphic design and digital drawing.
Refining the Craft: Grades 6-9 (Ages 11-15)
  • Portfolio Analysis - Reviewing and comparing art portfolios.
  • Medium & Style - Each student chooses a medium to work with and works on small projects.
  • Creative AI - Learn to achieve predefined outcomes from creative AI's, such as Leonardo.
Advanced Artistry: Grades 10-12 (Ages 15-18)
  • Crafting for Commercialization: Designing crafts with market potential, understanding pricing and cost.
  • Artistic Branding - Developing an artist's persona and self-presentation.
  • Exhibitions & Sales - Organizing art exhibits and understanding the business side.
  • Digital Presence - Building an online portfolio and using social media for promotion.
  • Introduction to Online Sales Platforms: Exploring platforms like Etsy, learning basics of online sales and marketing.
  • Fundamentals of Copyrighting & Licensing
